Section 2: The Nutrition Strategy & Meal Plan

To gain muscle, he needs to eat enough protein and calories. We'll aim for a *slight* calorie surplus (around 2,700 kcal) to fuel workouts and muscle repair without adding significant fat.

Daily Macro Calorie Split

This is how those calories break down. Protein is high to build muscle, fats are essential for hormones, and carbs provide energy for training.

Sample 4-Meal Plan (Approximate)

Meal 1: Breakfast

Focus: Energy & Protein

  • **Oatmeal:** 1 cup dry rolled oats (Carbs)
  • **Eggs:** 4 scrambled egg whites + 1 whole egg (Protein, Fat)
  • **Fruit:** 1 large banana or berries (Carbs)
  • **Fat:** 1 tbsp nut butter mixed in oats (Fat)

Meal 2: Lunch

Focus: Sustained Energy

  • **Protein:** 150g grilled Chicken Breast (Protein)
  • **Carb:** 1 cup cooked Brown Rice or Quinoa (Carb)
  • **Veg:** Large side salad with mixed greens (Micronutrients)
  • **Dressing:** 1 tbsp Olive Oil (Fat)

Meal 3: Dinner

Focus: Recovery & Fullness

  • **Protein:** 150g baked Salmon or Chicken Breast (Protein, Fat)
  • **Carb:** 2 medium Sweet Potatoes (Carb)
  • **Veg:** Steamed Broccoli or Asparagus (Micronutrients)
  • **Salt/Pepper** for seasoning.

Meal 4: Snack / PWO

Focus: Quick Protein (No Shake)

  • **Dairy Protein:** 1 cup Cottage Cheese or 1.5 cups Greek Yogurt (Protein)
  • **Carb:** A few handfuls of dried Cereal or a piece of fruit (Quick Carb)
  • **Fat:** 10 Almonds or Walnuts (Fat)

Section 3: The "Ease-In" 4-Day Workout

This is the key to avoiding the painful soreness you mentioned. For the first 2 weeks, the goal is *acclimatization*, not exhaustion. Weights should feel light, and he should stop 2-3 reps *before* failure.
